Part One

Who She
Used to
Be

"Everything I learned, I brought with me."

Carrie Carter is Fort Worth born and raised. Before she ever put passengers on a mini bus, she spent 25 years working at the heart of travel technology — beginning at Travelscape, the company that became the platform Expedia purchased to build itself into one of the world's largest travel brands.

From there her career took her across the industry: Expedia, Orbitz, 12 years in Las Vegas working in travel tech, then 12 more years at Sabre. Her roles spanned customer development, client relations, revenue management, marketing, and digital media. A wide-ranging, successful career by any measure.

"I was knocking on doors I thought would open. And all of a sudden — nope. That one's not gonna open. So now what?"

Then came the layoffs. Two of them, within three years. The first took a full year before she landed another position. The second came after she built and developed systems at a new company, trained the entire team to use them, and was told — essentially — thank you, we've got it from here.

Both felt like endings. Both turned out to be redirections.

Part Two

What She
Built

"Fort Worth had a gap. And I was the one to fill it."

Cowtown Tour Company is a guided tour experience based in Fort Worth, Texas. Carrie operates a mini bus with longhorn horns on the front, holds 11 passengers, and takes groups all over the city — well beyond the Stockyards stereotype — to show visitors what Fort Worth is truly made of.

Tours run every day. Barbecue and breweries. Burgers and breweries. Margarita tours. Private custom experiences where clients describe their group, their time, and their vision — and Carrie builds the itinerary around them.

"I provide memories for people. Experiences they will never forget. That's what I do."

Passengers come from across the country and around the world. Sometimes three or four different states are represented on a single bus. Strangers get on. Something else gets off. And with the 2026 FIFA World Cup coming to Fort Worth, Carrie is positioned at exactly the right moment for her city's biggest stage yet.

Part Three

Why She
Built It

"Life is too short to not benefit the people around you."

After the second layoff, Carrie entered a season of quiet time — reflection, prayer, and soul searching. She turned to ChatGPT and Claude, put in what she knew, what she believed in, and what she was good at, and asked for help thinking through what was next.

From that conversation, she began to shape an idea. She brought it to friends. Their reaction was immediate: oh my gosh, it's perfect. That confirmation gave her the green light.

"Fort Worth is my hometown. I was born and raised here. This business is love made into a product."

The city she had grown up in had a gap — no tour company showing people the full Fort Worth beyond the obvious landmarks. She saw it. She had 25 years of travel industry experience to back her up. And she had a servant's heart that had always been oriented toward making other people's lives better. Cowtown Tour Company was the only logical answer.

Part Four

How She
Did It

"I molded the business into what it is now."

The idea did not arrive as a single flash of inspiration. It came through a process — reflection, AI-assisted brainstorming, conversations with friends, and a willingness to sit with uncertainty long enough to let clarity emerge.

Carrie used ChatGPT and Claude as thinking partners when she hit a wall during her job search. She input her experience, her values, and her beliefs and let the output give her something to chew on. From there she molded the concept into Cowtown Tour Company.

"AI is here and it's not going anywhere. I used it when I didn't know what to do next — and it helped me find my next."

She officially launched in September 2024. First tours ran that same month. Tours run every day now. Growth is driven by word of mouth, social media video sharing, and the kind of experience that passengers cannot stop talking about after they get off the bus.

Carrie also attends 1 Million Cups Fort Worth every Wednesday morning — a weekly gathering for entrepreneurs and founders that keeps her connected, accountable, and in community with other builders in the city she loves.

Lessons & Wisdom

What the Seeker Needs to Hear

Advice to her past self

"Always find the silver lining. Even on the bad days, there is good. The layoffs that felt like endings were redirections all along."

What success means now

"Providing memories. Experiences people will never forget. The look on someone's face when they fall in love with Fort Worth for the first time."

To someone afraid to start

"Life is too short to not have fun and to not benefit the people around you. Find the silver lining and start building toward it."

What had to change

"That a closed door is a rejection. If a door doesn't open, there is a reason. Something else is waiting. I'm now on the path I was always supposed to be on."

On resilience

"Resilience is not optional. You will hit walls. The lesson is not to force them — it is to trust that the right door is somewhere else and keep moving."

What keeps her going

"The people on the bus. The pictures, the laughs, the connections between strangers. Leading with a servant's heart — how can I make other people's lives better?"
