Part One

Who She
Used to
Be

"I've had a really big life."

Lena Killion is a theater major who went into construction. A stay-at-home mother for 11 years who became a competitive bodybuilder. An aerospace procurement specialist at Bell Helicopter who ended up inside one of DFW's largest funeral homes. She has never done anything halfway — and her life proves it.

She won second place her first time on a bodybuilding stage at age 35. Came back a year later with a custom costume and a full afro and won first place in two categories. She has been featured in Glamour Magazine. She has been ballroom dancing. She has spent 30+ years in sales across more industries than most people touch in a lifetime.

"I'm competitive in everything I do. I might not speak on it. But I'm going to win."

Through construction, aerospace, single motherhood, and divorce — through raising three sons and building relationships across every room she has ever walked into — Lena Killion has always been the person other people lean on. That did not change when she started her own company. It became the whole point.

Part Two

What She
Built

"I take the power away from the funeral home."

MLK Pre Arrangements is a funeral pre-planning and pre-need specialist company. Lena is not a funeral home. She is not corporate. She works with approximately 40 funeral home locations across Texas and multiple states as an independent broker — giving families options, pricing transparency, and a plan before they ever need one.

Pre-need plans through MLK have no interest, no down payment, and flexible payment terms of three, five, or ten years. Pricing locks in at today's rates — regardless of when death occurs. If a client passes away while still making payments, the remaining balance is waived.

"I saved one family $8,300 on a headstone. Delivered it in three weeks. That's what I do."

She is a Licensed Pre-Need Agent and Commissioned Notary — licensed in Texas, Illinois, California, Florida, Pennsylvania, and additional states. She also handles medical power of attorney, statutory durable power of attorney, and HIPAA authorization — all at no extra charge, notarized on the spot while sitting with the family.

She does not work with corporate funeral chains. She works for the family.

Part Three

Why She
Built It

"My grandma was a beautiful example of how we should all live our lives."

When Lena's grandmother passed, she left nothing behind but love. No financial burden. No arguments at the funeral home. No family members scrambling to figure out what she would have wanted. She had pre-planned everything — life insurance, a pre-need arrangement, all decisions made in advance.

Lena watched that quiet, dignified exit and said: this is how we should all live. That became the mission. And then a headhunter called about a funeral industry job Lena did not want. She said no. The headhunter called back two months later. Lena heard it as a sign from her grandmother and took the job.

"Funeral homes catch you at your worst — at your lowest — and charge accordingly. I watched families be turned away because they couldn't afford the services. I couldn't tell them where to go. I would have lost my job. So I left."

She walked out of that corporate funeral home without a plan. She called it irresponsible. It was the best decision she ever made — and the beginning of everything that became MLK Pre Arrangements.

Part Four

How She
Did It

"Immerse yourself in the business before you try to build one."

Lena did not start by writing a business plan. She started by going inside — immersing herself completely in the funeral industry, learning how it operates from the inside out, building relationships with funeral homes and vendors, and earning appointments as an independent agent one by one.

Getting appointed by a funeral home, she says, is like asking for their hand in marriage. You have to earn the trust. And she has earned it across 40 locations and multiple states — including appointments that allow her to serve veterans on fixed incomes across Texas with flexible payment structures designed specifically for them.

"A radio segment I recorded in October aired months later. At 5:40 in the morning my phone started ringing — back to back to back. Two days in a row. When people know better, they do better."

She hired a virtual assistant to handle veteran outreach. She is pursuing B2B packages with businesses. She is pushing into churches for community education. She is hosting her own monthly speed networking events in Fort Worth. Lena Killion is not waiting for permission. She is building the platform herself — one conversation, one family, one plan at a time.

Lessons & Wisdom

What the Seeker Needs to Hear

Advice to her past self

"Sit with yourself. Ask whether there is something you are passionate about that you can create for the betterment of other people. Then get up and go do something."

What success means now

"Sitting across from a family and saying — you've got your ducks in a row. Leaving people more protected than I found them."

To someone afraid to start

"Network. Get out there. Go somewhere close to your home where people are gathering. Say out loud what you are looking for. Do not fear. Something good is out there."

What had to change

"That I needed someone else's company to do meaningful work. Once I saw what I could do on my own — for families that a corporate employer never would help — there was no going back."

On faith

"God is my foundation. No matter how far out I go, there is always a tether to come back to. Find something you believe in so deeply that no one can break it. Hold on."

What keeps her going

"My personality is attached to everything I do. There is not another Lena Killion. There is not another MLK Pre Arrangements. Put your full self in — it cannot be replicated."
